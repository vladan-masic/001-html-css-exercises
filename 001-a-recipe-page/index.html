Homemade Yogurt (Classic & Creamy)

Here’s a simple, reliable homemade yogurt recipe you can make with basic ingredients, plus tips so it turns out thick and tasty every time.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter (4 cups) milk
    • Whole milk = creamier yogurt
    • Low-fat works too, just slightly thinner
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt (with live active cultures)
    • Store-bought is fine, as long as it’s unsweetened

Instructions

  1. Pour the milk into a pot and heat it slowly over medium heat. Bring it to 85–90°C (185–195°F). Stir occasionally so it doesn’t scorch.
  2. Remove the pot from heat and let the milk cool to 43–46°C (110–115°F).
  3. Put 2 tablespoons of yogurt in a small bowl. Add a little warm milk and mix until smooth. Pour this mixture back into the pot and stir gently.
  4. Pour the milk into a jar or bowl. Cover loosely (lid or cloth). Wrap it in a towel and place it somewhere warm. Let it sit for 6–10 hours. 6 hours = mild and soft. 8–10 hours = tangier and thicker.
  5. Move it to the fridge. Chill for at least 3 hours. This stops fermentation and firms it up.
